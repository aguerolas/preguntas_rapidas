# executive


control deficiency:
The legacy applications of Santander Consumers operate on technology solutions that are not aligned with the organization’s current technology and security standards. Additionally, the lack of available architecture documentation and technical design information for these applications prevents validating whether existing controls remain adequate. This represents a missing control, as the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and operational continuity of the applications cannot be fully assessed against current technical baselines.


risk description:
“A potential risk of unauthorized disclosure of sensitive financial information, interruption of critical business processes (Telebanking, Collections, Electronic Factoring), or regulatory non-compliance is identified due to the use of legacy technology components that are not aligned with current technology standards, and the limited documentation available to determine whether these components remain adequately supported and secured prior to the transition to the Santander Consumer corporate ecosystem.”

Required Evidence for Closure
“Evidence confirming the decommissioning of the Santander APMs, including revocation of user access, deactivation of application functionality, and decommissioning of the associated servers/infrastructure once the TSA period concludes.”




risk statement 
“The overall risk of this assessment is rated as Medium. The residual risk rating is assessed as Medium based on the completion of risk assessment activities, given that the legacy applications manage confidential data with highly valuable integrity, high availability, and high continuity requirements. This risk is scoped to Santander Consumer’s business applications, as Santander Consumers does not process or store Scotiabank banking information, nor does it impact any banking functionality or service. Additionally, the applications under evaluation are supported by internal business policies, and the business lines are maintained separately from the Scotiabank Perú’s environment. In conclusion, Santander Consumers’ applications have no linkage to Scotiabank Perú’s banking applications. The use of technology solutions that are not aligned with current technology standards, combined with the limited availability of architecture and technical documentation for these legacy applications, could result in unauthorized exposure of sensitive client financial information, interruption of critical business processes, or non-compliance with regulatory standards.”

Background
What does this system or service provide?

Currently, the financial institution Crediscotia has been sold to Santander Consumer, another banking entity, after ceasing to be part of the Scotiabank group. As part of this transition, the institution’s requirements and projects focus exclusively on regulatory and cybersecurity matters. In this context, Santander Consumers Bank (SCB) is adapting the solution to the infrastructure and architecture of Crediscotia, with the goal of aligning its technological capabilities to the group’s standards. As part of this process, a set of legacy applications within Crediscotia has been identified.

These legacy applications operate exclusively within Santander Consumer’s business environment, handling processes and data that are entirely separate from Scotiabank’s banking infrastructure. The applications neither process nor store any banking information belonging to Scotiabank, nor do they interact with or affect any banking functionality or service. Their scope is strictly bounded to Crediscotia’s internal business operations, governed and supported by Santander Consumer’s internal policies and compliance frameworks, ensuring a clear and controlled separation between the financial institution’s ecosystem and the bank’s environment.

Given that these applications manage critical business processes and handle sensitive client financial information, it is essential to conduct a security assessment to determine their current posture, identify possible vulnerabilities, and establish the necessary controls to guarantee their confidentiality, integrity, availability, and operational continuity within the new corporate ecosystem.
